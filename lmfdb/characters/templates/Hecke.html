A Hecke Character is a character \( \chi=\mathcal{I}_{K}\to \C^\times \) on the ideals \( \mathfrak a\) of a number field $K$.

In the LMFDB, Hecke characters are described as triples \( (\chi_f, \chi_\infty, \chi_{\mathcal Cl}) \) of {{ KNOWL('chars.hecke.dirichlet_character', title='Dirichlet character') }} \(\chi_f\) of some {{ KNOWL('chars.hecke.modulus', title='modulus') }} \(\mathfrak m \), {{ KNOWL('chars.hecke.infinity_type', title='infinity type') }} \( \chi_{\infty} \) and {{ KNOWL('chars.hecke.classgroupchar', title='class group character') }} \( \chi_{\mathcal C_l} \).

Hecke characters can be described as couples \( \left(\chi_P, \chi_{\mathcal C_l}\right) \) where

  • \( \chi_P \) is a character on the group \( P(K) \) of principal ideals \( (\alpha), \alpha\in\mathcal O_K \)
  • and \( \chi_{\mathcal C_l} : \mathcal C_l(K) \to \C^\times \) is a class group character.

The principal characters \( \chi_P \) can in turn be described as products \( \chi_P = \chi_{f}\chi_{\infty} \) where

  • \( \chi_f : \left(\mathcal{O}_K/\mathfrak m\right)^\times \to \C^\times \) is a (finite order) Dirichlet character defined modulo a modulus \( \mathfrak m \);
  • \( \chi_{\infty} : K\otimes \R \to \C^\times \) is a continuous character on the archimedean embeddings of \( K \);
such that in order to define a character on the ideals these characters satisfy the compatibility condition \( \chi_f(\alpha) \times \chi_{\infty}(\alpha) = 1 \) for all units \( \alpha \in \mathcal{O}_K^\times \). {# \equiv 1\mod \mathfrak m. #}
